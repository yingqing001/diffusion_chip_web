Dall-EM: Generative AI with Diffusion Models for New Design Space Discovery and Target-to-Electromagnetic Structure Synthesis

Yingqing Guo1, Emir Ali Karahan2, Zihao Li3, Zijian Shao4, Zaixi Zhang5, Mengdi Wang6, Kaushik Sengupta7
Princeton University, USA
Paper arXiv

Dall-EM introduces generative AI with diffusion models for rapid synthesis of arbitrary shaped electromagnetic structures from target S-parameters.

TL;DR

Problem: RF and mmWave circuit design relies on designer experience with time-intensive simulations and limited parameterized templates that restrict design space.

Our Innovation: First-ever application of diffusion models for electromagnetic (EM) structure synthesis from target S-parameters, enabling arbitrary shaped structures beyond traditional templates.

Key Results:

  • Design convergence in seconds vs weeks with traditional methods
  • ~10x faster than prior predictive AI models
  • Successfully demonstrated with fabricated structures across 10-30 GHz
  • Achieved normalized L1 error of 0.065 for S-parameter matching

Problem & Motivation

Traditional RF/mmWave circuit design faces significant challenges: (1) Designer experience-driven process with tedious manual steps, (2) Limited to pre-defined parameterized templates that restrict design space, (3) Time and resource-intensive EM simulations and iterative optimization, (4) No set templates for complex spectral responses. While optimization methods exist, they require minutes to hours for synthesis, limiting design exploration.

Dataset properties showing 18×18 pixelated EM structures on PCB substrate, simulated across 10-30 GHz for FR-3, satellite and low FR-2 bands.

Diffusion-Based EM Synthesis

Our approach applies conditional diffusion models to EM structure generation. The method: (1) Represents EM structures as 18×18 binary-valued grids (images), (2) Trains a U-Net with cross-attention mechanism on 145K simulations, (3) Uses S21 magnitude (21-dimensional vector) as conditioning input, (4) Employs score function approximation to guide denoising from random noise to valid EM structure, (5) Leverages DDIM scheduler for inference in ~25 seconds. The model learns the conditional distribution P(x|y) where x is the EM structure and y is the target S-parameters.

Design Examples & Results

The diffusion model successfully generates diverse filter designs: (1) Sharp transition low-pass filters (10-15 GHz pass, 16-30 GHz stop), (2) Band-stop filters at 14-16 GHz, (3) Band-pass filters with different center frequencies, (4) Narrow-band filters (15.5-16.5 GHz). Training-free guidance techniques optimize for ideal responses like brick-wall filters. The approach achieves normalized L1 error of 0.065 compared to CNN predictor baseline of 0.020.

Design examples: (a) Low pass filter with sharp transition, (b)-(c) Band stop and band pass filters at 15 GHz, (d) Band pass at different frequency.

Experimental Validation

Fabricated structures on Rogers PCB substrate demonstrate successful synthesis. Measurement results show: (1) Good correlation between simulation and measurement despite PCB process variations, (2) Multiple synthesis runs produce different structures for same target due to stochastic nature, (3) Successful band-pass operation at 14-16 GHz and narrow-band at 15.5-16.5 GHz, (4) Robustness to process variations validates the synthesis approach. This represents the first demonstration of EM structure synthesis with directed diffusion models.
